Expectation task

Before learning about your proposer type (Alone, AI-System assistance, Autonomous AI-System) and making an offer to your Responder, you will state your expectations about how your Responder will decide for each possible Allocation and proposer type. Thus, you will state your expectations for 18 different scenarios. You can earn an additional fixed bonus payment of 250 coins.

For all 18 scenarios, you will see the same table with two columns and 21 rows (see “Decision table” on the next page). Each row shows you two possible probabilities of receiving the bonus. Your job is to choose one of the 21 rows.

Which one of the two probabilities given in each row will be selected to determine your bonus depends on the Responder’s decision. If the Responder chose a minimum split accepting less or the same amount of money compared to the Allocation in your respective scenario, the left column will determine your probability of receiving the fixed reward. If the Responder chose a minimum split only accepting more than the Allocation in your respective scenario, the right column will determine your probability of receiving the fixed reward.


When choosing a row, note that:

  • the first rows offer you a higher probability of receiving the bonus in case the Responder’s minimum offer is lower or equal to their income according to the Allocation you are making a decision for.

  • the last rows offer you a higher probability of receiving the bonus in case the Responder’s minimum offer is higher than their income according to the Allocation you are making a decision for.


Thus, for a given Allocation, if you think the Responder will ask for at most as much money as indicated in the Allocation, you should focus more on the probabilities shown in the left column.

At the end of the experiment, a computer will randomly choose one of the six scenarios that correspond to your proposer type. The randomly chosen scenario determines whether you receive the additional 250 coins. Thus, right now, every scenario could be the one that determines your bonus.

Imagine you would choose a row for Allocation 3 to state your expectations regarding the Responder’s action. Allocation 3 assigns you a split of 300 Coins. Depending on your expectation regarding the Responder’s minimum offer (demand), the left or right column is more likely to become relevant in determining your probability to receive the bonus. Accordingly, you may want to adjust your chosen row. Assuming that you are expecting the Responder ...

  • to definitely ask for at most the 200 coins of Allocation 3 (i.e. 200 or less), you may want to choose a higher row, i.e. a lower row number. This is because you expect the left column to be more likely and the probability of reward is much higher for the lower row numbers. In case your expectation is wrong and the Responder asks for more than the 200 Coins determined by Allocation 3, the right column determines your bonus, and the lower row number gives you a very low probability of receiving the 250 bonus coins.

  • to definitely ask for more than the 200 coins of Allocation 3, you may want to choose a lower row, i.e. a higher row number. This is because you expect the right column to be more likely and the probability of reward is much higher for the higher row numbers. In case your expectation is wrong and the Responder asks for at most the 200 Coins determined by Allocation 3 (i.e. 200 or less), the left column determines your bonus, and the higher row number gives a very low probability of receiving the 250 bonus coins.

Overall, if you are very uncertain about whether the Responder asks for more, less or exactly the 200 Coins of Allocation 3, you may want to choose a row number that’s closer to the middle of the 21 rows.

Compared to Allocation 3, for Allocation 1 it might be more likely for the Responder to ask more than the respective 0 Coins. Thus you may want to adjust your selection more in favor of the right column and choose higher row numbers.

Compared to Allocation 3, for Allocation 6 it might be more likely for the Responder to ask for 500 Coins or less. Thus you may want to adjust your selection more in favor of the left column and choose higher rows.
