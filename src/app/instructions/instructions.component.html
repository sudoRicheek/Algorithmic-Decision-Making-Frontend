Participants and Roles

In this HIT, you will play with three other participants. You and the other participants make your decisions separately, without knowing what the other one is doing.

Three of you are assigned the role of Proposer, the fourth one is assigned the role of Responder.


Responders decide which one of the three Proposers they want to approach:

  • One Proposer completes this task alone and without any help

  • One Proposer has an AI-System to assist them in making a decision

  • One Proposer is substituted by the AI-System that makes a decision on their behalf. The Proposer still receives the potential payoff from this HIT, but the decision is made by the AI-System autonomously.


The AI-System (Machine-Learning) was trained using prior interactions of comparable bargaining situations and participant personality data.

Responders will determine the minimum amount of Coins they are willing to accept from the Proposer by also choosing one of the 6 possible splits.

  • In case the split chosen by the Responder earns them equal or less than what the Proposer offered, both are paid according to the initial Proposer offer. The offered split is accepted.

  • In case the split chosen by the Responder earns them more than what the Proposer offered, both are paid nothing. The offered split is rejected.


Accordingly, the Responder chooses a minimum share of the split that needs to be met to accept a Proposer's offer.

Example 1:

  • A Proposer chose Allocation 2 and therefore decided to offer the Responder 4 Coins and to keep 16 Coins.

  • The Responder chose Allocation 3 and therefore decided to accept all splits that pay at least 8 Coins.

  • The Responder's demand exceeded the Proposer's offer.

  • The split is rejected, both participants receive nothing.


Example 2:

  • A Proposer chose Allocation 3 and therefore decided to offer the Responder 8 Coins and to keep 12 Coins.

  • The Responder chose Allocation 2 and therefore decided to accept all splits that pay at least 4 Coins.

  • The Responder's demand did not exceed the Proposer's offer.

  • The split is accepted, both participants are paid according to the Proposer's offer of Allocation 3.
